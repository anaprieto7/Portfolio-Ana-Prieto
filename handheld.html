Overview

Handheld PWA brings WEMALO warehouse operations to mobile devices. Instead of forcing operators to work from desktop UIs at fixed stations, the app lets them scan locations and products directly on the floor, with immediate visual and sound feedback.

My work spanned UX research, information architecture and UX/UI design ‚Äî always respecting existing backend rules while reshaping the interaction into a touch-first, sequential experience.

Solution

Scanning-centered experience

  • Sequential scanning flow
    Each product requires scanning its location immediately before, mirroring WEMALO‚Äôs core business rule.
  • Immediate visual & sound feedback
    Green/red states, vibration and concise messages guide operators without long instructions.

The app enforces the correct sequence implicitly: users simply follow the scanner and colors, not a written manual.

Mobile architecture

I grouped the app by operational task type, starting from a simple dashboard:

  • Dashboard ‚Äî entry point to all tasks.
  • Booking ‚Äî register goods receipts.
  • Picking ‚Äî unsorted and sorted picking flows.
  • Packing ‚Äî prepare parcels for shipment.
  • Relocation ‚Äî move products between locations.
  • Profile ‚Äî user and device context.

Grouping by task type reduces search time and keeps the mental model consistent with real work.

Test objective

Confirm that operators understood the ‚Äúscan location ‚Üí scan product‚Äù rule through the interface, and that visual/sound feedback was enough to guide them without extra training.

Method

  • Participants: 5 operators (3 experienced, 2 new).
  • Duration: ~30 minutes per session.
  • Context: Real warehouse environment with mobile devices and scanners.

Tasks evaluated:

  • Register products using the Booking flow.
  • Complete a Picking order with 3 items.
  • Pack a box using the Packing flow.

Handheld PWA didn‚Äôt just reduce errors and time ‚Äî it aligned human interaction with system rules, without additional training.

Key challenge

Each product had to be tied to a just-scanned location, without slowing operators down. The flow resets context after each assignment and uses strong feedback to prevent mistakes.
